Impact Jet Experiment - Fluid Mechanics

Impact Jet Force and Discharge Calculation

Aim

The aim of the impact jet experiment is to calculate the forces exerted by a jet of fluid and the discharge based on the mass, velocity, and area of the nozzle.

Process

The process involves measuring the velocity of a jet, calculating the mass, and determining the nozzle area. Using these parameters, we calculate the actual force exerted by the fluid and the theoretical force according to fluid mechanics equations.

Application

This experiment is used in various fields, including fluid mechanics, mechanical engineering, and civil engineering. It is important for understanding jet propulsion, hydraulic systems, and optimizing nozzle designs in industrial applications.

Setup

The experimental setup involves a jet of fluid directed at a surface. Key components include the fluid supply system, a nozzle, a measuring device for velocity, and a scale to measure the mass of the fluid. Accurate measurements are crucial for obtaining precise results.

Formulas Used:
1. Actual Force: F_actual = m * g
   Where:
   - m = mass (kg)
   - g = gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²)

2. Theoretical Force: F_theoretical = 2 * ρ * A * v²
   Where:
   - ρ = density of fluid (kg/m³)
   - A = area of nozzle (m²)
   - v = velocity (m/s)

3. Discharge: Q = A * v
   Where:
   - A = area of nozzle (m²)
   - v = velocity (m/s)
